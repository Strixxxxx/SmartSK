Based on my scan of the frontend and backend codebase, here is a step-by-step explanation of how the **Annual Project Cycle** works in smartSK. 

The process is designed as a rigid, state-driven workflow consisting of **14 sequential checkpoints** that guide Sangguniang Kabataan (SK) projects from inception (profiling) through execution and closure.

---

### Phase 1: Initialization (Backend Logic)
The process starts in the backend (`projectTracker.js`) via the `POST /initialize-cycle` endpoint. 
1. **Trigger:** Only the **SK Chairperson (SKC)** can trigger this.
2. **Validation:** The system checks if the term duration is exactly 3 years, the fiscal year is within bounds, and no other active cycle exists.
3. **Creation:** It creates a new `projectCycles` record in the database starting at **Status ID 1**.
4. **Auto-Generation:** It automatically generates initial templates/batches for the **CBYDP** (Comprehensive Barangay Youth Development Plan) and the **ABYIP** (Annual Barangay Youth Investment Program) and stores them via Azure Blob Storage.

---

### Phase 2: The 14-Step Checkpoint Tracker (Frontend & Backend Synchronization)
Once initialized, the cycle progresses through 14 distinct checkpoints defined in the frontend (`ProjectTrackerList.tsx`) and controlled by the backend.

**Step 1: Youth Profiling**
* The initial state. Gathering data to understand the youth demographic.

**Step 2: CBYDP Drafting**
* The SK Council works on drafting the 3-year plan. 
* **Validation:** Before advancing, the backend checks Azure Blob Storage to ensure the Local Youth Development Plan (LYDP) document is uploaded.

**Step 3: CBYDP SK Session**
* **Scheduling:** The SKC schedules an official finalization meeting (`POST /schedule-meeting`).
* **Attendance:** All active term members must check in their attendance (`POST /submit-self-attendance` or `POST /submit-attendance`). 
* **Progression:** Once 100% of the active council is marked as present, the system *automatically* advances to the next step.

### Key System Mechanisms (Cross-Cutting Features)

1. **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):**
   * **SKC (Chairperson):** Holds primary control over advancing checkpoints, scheduling meetings, and finalizing plans.
   * **BCPT (Barangay Captain):** Primarily an observer with specific approval gates (like Budget Validation at Step 5) and emergency override powers.
   * **Standard SK Members:** Participate heavily in attendance and document viewing.

2. **Real-time Synchronization (WebSockets):**
   * Whenever a status changes, a meeting is scheduled, or attendance is logged, the backend fires `broadcastToRoom()` via WebSockets. The frontend listens for these events to update the progress bar without requiring a page refresh.

3. **Time-Based Warnings & Compliance:**
   * The frontend continuously calculates the "age" of a status (`getStatusAge`). 
   * It enforces the **7/10 rule** for SK Sessions/Approvals (Warning at 7 days, Urgent at 10 days) and a **15/30 rule** for standard phases to ensure compliance with JMC 2019 legal limits.

4. **Document Gates (Azure Blob Storage):**

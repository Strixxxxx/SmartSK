Diagnosis
The new error is a 404 Not Found, which is a server-side routing issue.

Here are the key pieces of information from the log:

Error Code: POST .../api/login 404 (Not Found)
Response Body: <pre>Cannot POST /api/login</pre>
Let's break this down:

The Good News: The CORS error is gone. Your frontend is now successfully communicating with your backend server on Azure.

The New Problem: The error message Cannot POST /api/login is coming directly from your Express.js application. It means that a POST request arrived at the server for the /api/login path, but the server could not find any route handler configured to manage it.

Root Cause: While your main.js file does have a line app.use('/api/login', loginRouter);, the overall structure of your routing and middleware is overly complex and has introduced a bug. The way you are using the custom protectRoutesExcept middleware is unconventional and makes the request flow difficult to manage.

Specifically, your protectRoutesExcept middleware is applied after the public routes it's supposed to be excluding. This creates a confusing and fragile setup. For example, on the /api/logout route, your authentication middleware is being applied twice—once by protectRoutesExcept and again in the route definition itself.

While that specific bug doesn't directly break /api/login, it's a strong indicator that the routing logic is flawed. It's highly likely this complex structure is causing the /api/login request to not be handled correctly, leading to the 404 error.

Solution
The solution is to refactor the routing and middleware structure in d:\Projects\Projects\smartSK\backend\main.js to follow a clearer, more standard Express.js pattern. This will simplify the code, fix the underlying bugs, and make your application much more reliable and easier to debug.

Here is the recommended approach:

Group Routes by Protection Level: Instead of using a complex exclusion-based middleware, you should structure your main.js file to first define all public routes, then apply a single authentication "gate," and then define all protected routes.

Simplify Middleware Application:

Register all your public routes (like /api/login, /api/forgotpassword, etc.) first.
After all public routes, register your authMiddleware once with app.use(authMiddleware). This will protect every route defined after it.
Remove the custom protectRoutesExcept function entirely.
Remove the now-redundant authMiddleware from individual protected route definitions (like /api/logout).
This refactoring will create a clean, linear request-response flow that is standard in Express applications and will resolve the routing conflict that is causing the 404 Not Found error.
